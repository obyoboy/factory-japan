What to Do Before Your Residence Card Expires While Working at a Factory in Japan

Last updated: 2026-04-23  |  6 min read

Your residence card (在留カード / zairyū kādo) has an expiry date printed on it — and missing that deadline can cost you your job, your visa status, and even your right to stay in Japan. Here is what every factory worker needs to know and do before that date arrives.

1. Understand What the Residence Card Is and Why It Matters

The residence card (在留カード / zairyū kādo) is the official document that proves you are legally allowed to live and work in Japan. It is issued by the Immigration Services Agency and must be carried with you at all times. The card shows your visa status (called 在留資格 / zairyū shikaku), the permitted period of stay, and any work restrictions that apply to your status.

As a factory worker, your employer is also legally responsible for confirming that your residence card is valid. If your card expires while you are employed, your company could face penalties — and you could lose your job on the spot. Do not wait until the last minute.

Tip Check the expiry date on your residence card right now. The date is printed in the format YYYY年MM月DD日 on the front of the card under 在留期間満了日 (zairyū kikan manryōbi).

2. Know How Early You Can Apply for Renewal

You are allowed to apply for a renewal of your residence card up to 3 months before the expiry date. Most immigration experts recommend applying at least 2 to 3 months in advance to give yourself enough buffer time in case of delays or document issues.

Do not apply too early — applications submitted more than 3 months before the expiry date will not be accepted. Mark your calendar so you know exactly when the 3-month window opens.

3. Gather the Required Documents

The documents you need depend on your visa status, but for most factory workers on a Technical Intern Training (技能実習 / ginō jisshū) or Specified Skilled Worker (特定技能 / tokutei ginō) visa, the standard documents include:

  • Your current residence card (original)
  • Your passport (valid, with at least 6 months remaining)
  • Application form for extension of period of stay (在留期間更新許可申請書)
  • Recent photo (4cm × 3cm, taken within the last 3 months)
  • Certificate of employment or a letter from your employer (在職証明書)
  • Documents proving your activities in Japan (pay slips, attendance records)
  • Tax documents (住民税 / jūminzei certificate, or withholding tax slip)

Your company's 総務 (sōmu / general affairs department) or a supervising organization (監理団体 / kanri dantai) may help you gather these. Always ask — it is their responsibility to assist you.

Tip Ask your 班長 (hanchō / team leader) or 総務担当者 (sōmu tantōsha / HR officer) at your factory who handles visa paperwork. Many companies have someone who manages this process for foreign workers specifically.

4. Submit Your Application at the Right Place

Renewal applications are submitted at the Regional Immigration Services Bureau (出入国在留管理局 / shutsunyūkoku zairyū kanri kyoku) nearest to where you live. You cannot send applications by mail for most status types — you must appear in person, or have your employer or legal representative appear on your behalf.

Before going, check the bureau's official website for current wait times and whether you need an appointment. Some offices allow walk-in applications; others require booking. Bring all your documents in order and arrive early — lines can be very long.

Warning Do not confuse the Immigration Bureau with the City Hall (役所 / yakusho). City Hall handles your residence registration (住民票 / jūminhyō), but visa renewal must go to the Immigration Bureau. Going to the wrong office wastes time and could push you past your deadline.

5. What Happens After You Apply

When you submit your application before your card expires, you receive a stamped notice in your passport that says your application is under review. During this period — even if your old card has technically expired — you can legally continue to live and work in Japan while waiting for the decision. This is called the 特例期間 (tokurei kikan / special grace period), and it lasts up to 2 months after your original expiry date.

Keep the stamped passport and your old residence card with you at all times during this period. If police or your employer asks to see your visa status, you can show these documents as proof that your renewal is in progress.

6. If Your Application Is Approved

Once approved, you will receive a notification (usually a postcard or letter at your registered address) to collect your new residence card. You must bring your passport and the notification to the Immigration Bureau to pick it up in person. Check the new card carefully — confirm that your name, date of birth, and status are all correct before you leave the office.

Update your workplace's HR (総務 / sōmu) with your new card details as soon as you receive it. They are legally required to record the new expiry date in their files.

Tip Take a photo of your new residence card (both front and back) and save it securely on your phone as a backup. If you lose your physical card, having a photo can help speed up the replacement process.

7. If Your Application Is Denied

If your renewal is denied, you will receive a written notice explaining the reason. You have a limited time — usually 3 months from the denial date — to either appeal the decision, apply for a different visa status, or prepare to leave Japan.

Do not ignore a denial notice. Speak immediately with your supervising organization (監理団体 / kanri dantai), your employer's 総務担当者 (sōmu tantōsha), or a registered immigration lawyer (行政書士 / gyōsei shoshi or 弁護士 / bengoshi). Options exist, but time is critical.

Warning Working in Japan after your residence card has expired — without a pending renewal application — is an overstay violation. Penalties include deportation, a ban from re-entering Japan (sometimes for 5 or 10 years), and criminal charges. There is no exception for being busy or not knowing the deadline.

8. Special Cases: Changing Jobs or Status

If you are changing factories or switching from Technical Intern Training to Specified Skilled Worker status, the renewal process is different. In addition to extending your period of stay, you may also need to file a 在留資格変更許可申請 (zairyū shikaku henkō kyoka shinsei / application to change residence status). This is a more complex process — start it at least 3 months in advance and get support from your employer or supervising organization early.

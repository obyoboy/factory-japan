How to Read Your Japanese Factory Payslip
(and Understand Overtime Pay)

Last updated: March 2026  |  10 min read

Your first Japanese payslip looks like a wall of numbers and kanji. You expected a certain amount — but what landed in your bank account is noticeably less. You are not alone. Almost every foreign factory worker goes through this moment of confusion. This guide walks you through every section of a Japanese payslip, explains what gets deducted and why, and shows you exactly how overtime pay is calculated so you can check the numbers yourself.

1. What Is a Payslip in Japan?

In Japan, your payslip is called a 給与明細書 (kyūyo meisaisho) — literally "salary detail document." By law, your employer must provide one every pay period. Most factories pay monthly (月給 / gekkyū), though some dispatch (派遣 / haken) arrangements pay weekly or bi-weekly.

The payslip is usually divided into three sections:

  • 支給 (shikyū) — Earnings: everything your employer pays you
  • 控除 (kōjo) — Deductions: everything taken out before you receive the money
  • 差引支給額 (sashihiki shikyūgaku) — Net pay: the actual amount deposited into your bank account
Tip: Take a photo of every payslip you receive and store it somewhere safe. If there is ever a dispute about your wages, you will need these records.

Warning: 住民税 (jūmin zei / resident tax) is not deducted in your first year of working in Japan. It kicks in from June of the following year. Many workers are caught off guard when their payslip suddenly shows a new deduction in June. Budget for it in advance.

Tip: Keep a personal log of your working hours every day. Compare your log to the overtime hours shown on your payslip each month. Discrepancies of even a few hours per month add up to significant lost wages over a year.

5. Haken vs. Direct Hire: How Payslips Differ

Item Direct Hire (直接雇用 / chokusetsu koyō) Dispatch / Haken (派遣)

Employer on payslipFactory company nameAgency company name
Social insuranceThrough factoryThrough agency (if full-time haken)
Bonus (賞与 / shōyo)Often includedRarely included

Warning: As a haken worker, you cannot directly negotiate wages with the factory. Your contract is with the agency. If you believe your overtime is being underpaid, raise it with your agency's 担当者 (tantōsha / person in charge).

6. "Why Is My Pay So Low?" — A Checklist

# What to Check Where to Find It on the Payslip
1Is your 基本給 (base salary) correct?支給 section, first line

8. Final Thoughts: You Have the Right to Understand Your Pay

Reading your payslip is not just about checking numbers — it is about understanding your legal rights as a worker in Japan. Start by confirming your base hourly rate and comparing your recorded hours to the overtime figure on your payslip each month. You work hard on the factory floor. Make sure you are paid correctly for every hour of it.
